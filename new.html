  • My Story

    My favorite thing in the world is a great adventure, whether it be a week-long backpacking trek or the complex work of building a company. To me, they go hand in hand. When I'm not working with my team in New York City, you'll find me exploring some remote wilderness spot.
     
    I started ReWork after years of searching for meaningful work, a journey that lasted from my college graduation in 2008 until the summer of 2011 when I attended the Unreasonable Institute and had the details of our company vision come into focus. My journey has involved all sorts of jobs - from building rain barrels to installing energy-efficient insulation in homes, to leading backcountry trail crews in US national parks and ecotourism trips to Latin America, to securing $1.5M+ in funding for nonprofits  - and showed me how difficult yet worthwhile the pursuit of meaningful work is.
     
    ReWork is my way of making that pursuit easier for everyone else.
     
    My team and I believe that the world is hungry for more meaningful work, and there's never been a better time for that to become the prevailing paradigm through which people view their careers.

  • My Work

     My work is in career design, recruiting, & workforce engagement in the purpose economy.

    ReWork

    Co-Founder & CEO
    The most talented professionals want meaning in their work.
    ReWork helps mission-driven companies and organizations recruit and engage exceptional professionals. We are proud to have run over 100 searches for clients such as Acumen, GOOD/Corps, Echoing Green, the Center for Financial Services Innovation, 4.0 Schools, Population Services International, Moneythink, the B Team, and Landmark Health - and many other nonprofits, social enterprises, foundations, and mission-driven brands.
     
    Our innovation team has helped organizations like The United Nations, Rally Software, Schneider Electric, and the US Green Building Council deepen their engagement with their employees and constituents alike.
     
    ReWork is a 2011 Unreasonable Venture, a member of the 2015 New York City Venture Fellows cohort, and has offices in New York City, Denver, and Boulder.
